Why this land

What doesn't
exist anymore.

Large private parcels surrounded by native primary forest, with ocean views and 300 meters to the beach? They don't get listed in Pavones anymore. There aren't any left.

What makes this one rarer still: a decade of careful stewardship. Active wildlife corridors stretching into the wild La Hierba zone — sloths, tamanduas, coatis, ocelots, jaguarundis, scarlet macaws and toucans pass through. Two natural creeks bordering the land. Spring-fed potable water on site.

This isn't land waiting to be tamed. It's a thriving ecosystem already in balance — ready to host something beautiful without erasing what makes it worth coming for.
